# Elderly Care Website Design Guidelines

## Core Philosophy: Calm, Confidence, Clarity
- Every element reduces anxiety and builds trust.
- Minimalism, clear hierarchy, and one primary action per screen.
- Calming color palette (soft blues, neutrals), human-centric imagery, organic motion.
- Large, well-spaced interactive elements for motor accessibility.

## Cognitive Ease
- Use minimal, clear layouts.
- Prioritize one main action per screen.
- Typography: Large, readable fonts (base 18px+), high contrast.

## Emotional Safety
- Colors: Soft blues, warm neutrals, gentle greens.
- Imagery: Real people, authentic moments.
- Motion: Slow, organic, reassuring (500ms+ transitions).

## Motor Accessibility
- Buttons and links: Large, well-spaced, easy to tap/click.
- Avoid crowded layouts and small touch targets.

## Accessibility as Premium Feature
- "Senior Mode" toggle: Prominent, easy to use.
- Senior Mode: Larger text, higher contrast, reduced motion, simplified layout.

## Trust Elements
- Social proof: Animated counters, real caregiver quotes, official-looking badges.
- Data protection: Visual cues for compliance (HIPAA/GDPR look).

## Soundless Design
- All feedback is visual: glows, shakes, morphs, highlights.
- No sound cues; silence is calming.

## Humanized AI
- AI features named with warmth ("Smart Companion").
- Visual: Soft orb, gentle breathing animation, no robotic icons.

## Advanced Touches
- Ambient animated gradients in backgrounds.
- Personalized greeting in hero/dashboard.
- Exit-intent comfort modal/banner.

---

_This guideline ensures every design decision supports calmness, trust, and accessibility for seniors and caregivers._
